This Program is Not For Everyone

Our in-person Computer Programming courses are designed for busy adults driven to boost their professional skills and, often, to change their career. Few Coding Schools offer the length and breadth of material that we do.

We are a Computer Programming School devoted to creating Web Application Developers with sound Computer Science skills. Our curriculum is designed to be completed in a dense, but realistic time frame of nine months.

By the end of the 9-month program, each student will have their own portfolio full of web applications, something which university graduates lack and employers desperately seek.

Do you have what it takes? The FVI Web Development team has created 8 tutorials for you to try out for free right now. Completing these tutorials will give you a taste of Web Development and will help you judge if web development is right for you.

What Does A Network Admininstrator Do?

As a Network Administrator, you will be able to validate your ability to handle day-to-day management of the server operating system, file structure, and directory services. You will also learn how to handle software distribution and updates, monitor servers, provide troubleshooting support, build and configure servers, implement auditing policy, perform scheduled vulnerability-assessment scans, and monitor logs for firewalls and intrusion-detection systems.

Which Skills Will I Gain?

At the completion of this program you will be qualified to manage, support, and troubleshoot information systems in a wide range of computing environments with Microsoft Windows Server 2012 and Linux. Additionally, the course will provide the concepts, commands, and practice required to configure Cisco switches and routers in multi-protocol Internet works. Finally, students will receive an introduction to 21st-century cloud-based systems administration on Azure and Amazon Web Services.

Will I Obtain Any Certifications?

Students will be equipped to pass the following certification exams: Network+, Security+ and Window Server Administration Fundamentals (MTA-365). In addition, the curriculum covers a portion of Cisco CCNA and CompTIA Cloud+.

How Is This Program Structured?

This course is based on lectures, discussions, demonstrations, exercises, and laboratory projects. Students perform all basic configuration procedures to build LAN and WAN interfaces for the most commonly used routing and routed protocols.

What Kind of Job Can I Get as a Graduate?

Graduates are qualified for high demand careers in Network Administration, IT security, and Cloud Computing, such as Security Engineer, Security Consultant, Network Administrator, or IA Technician/Manager.

As organizations move their IT systems into the cloud, the need for Network Administrators trained on cloud computing will increase. FVI’s career services team will assist you in finding a career in IT by working to match you to open positions, connect you to local employers, and ensure your resume and professional readiness are a match for any open positions in IT.

Curriculum

Learn the latest, most in-demand technologies and languages.

Become skilled in the most important technologies in network administration.

+

Weeks 1-6: Foundations of Front-end Programming.

During the first 6 weeks, we wil introduce you to each of the main skills you need in order to become a front-end Programmer. You will learn HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, JavaScript, jQuery, Git, Ajax programming, and build tools (Browserify, npm and Gulp).

Weeks 7-15: Advanced Front-end Programming

Our faculty of real industry pros takes you through more advanced front-end development techniques in an environment of project-based learning. You will learn techniques for using gradients, HTML5 media, HTML5 canvas, CSS transforms and perspective, and lots of event-driven and AJAX programming.

Weeks 16-21: Back-end Programming

You will build at least 6 full stack (both front-end and back-end) applications using NodeJS on the server side and MongoDB or SQL for the database.

Weeks 22-36: AngularJS, php and Beyond

During this last stretch, you will learn AngularJS, ReactJS, php, and become a WordPress pro. We we say WordPress, we're not talking about clicking around a GUI and installing themes; we're talking about harnessing the WordPress codex to build real database-driven apps on the back of WordPress. You will also learn Computer Science fundamentals and advanced Git techniques.

How We Compare to Other Schools

You don't have to give up your day job to take our classes.

+

Coding Bootcamps

The South Florida region offers only a handful of coding bootcamps, including Wyncode and Ironhack. These programs are often effective, but they take place during the work day and do not offer financial aid. FVI's technology programs take place during the evenings and therefore don't interfere with the 9-5 career schedule and financial aid can cover the majority of tuition costs.

Most importantly, the coding bootcamps are 320 total hours while FVI's program is 720 total hours. We think 320 hours is barely enough time to become a web developer, let alone a strong one. There are no shortcuts--you will become a strong web developer by the end of our program.

Computer Science Degrees from Universities

A computer science degree will give you an excellent understanding of computers and the internet, but it won't give you any coding skills to apply your knowledge. You won't have a portoflio of projects proving you can make stuff.

A developer's portfolio is the most important part of their resume. By the end of FVI's web developer program, you will have a rich portfolio full of varied, in-depth, relevant projects.

Online Programs

There are a few different online coding schools and websites that can take a beginner from amateur to intermediate at a low cost, and often for free. For example, Codecademy provides many good introductions to various programming languages for free, Team Tree House provides learning paths with videos and exercises for a low monthly fee, and Coursera provides a few online computer science courses from excellent universities, for free.

However, online programs are notorious for low completion rates. As a student in FVI's web developer program, you will be learning and coding with a professional web developer in the same room who can immediately answer any questions, look at your code, and offer you feedback and encouragement. Such a close student-mentor relationship cannot be found in online programs.

Financial Aid: Everyone Can Afford A Quality Education

Since we are an accredited institutuion, many students can afford our programs with grants, loans, easy payments and other ways to manage tuition costs. Few, if any Coding Boot Camps or Web Developer Schools can offer the amount of options we can offer in terms of financing.

Florida Vocational Institute maintains a staff of financial aid professionals to assist students in obtaining the financial assistance they require to meet their educational expenses. Available resources include federal grant and loan programs, and student loans from private lenders.

Federal assistance programs are administered through the U.S. Department of Education. Any U.S. citizen, national, or person in the United States for other than temporary reasons who is enrolled and accepted may apply for these programs. Most forms of financial assistance are available for each July 1-June 30 award period.

Every student considering an application for financial aid should request a copy of the current guide, Funding Your Education, published by the U.S. Department of Education. This important document can be obtained online from The United States Office of Federal Student Aid and will assist persons in understanding eligibility requirements.
